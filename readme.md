## Project Overview
This project focuses on **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of forest fire incidents in Brazil.  
Brazil experiences frequent wildfires, particularly in the Amazon and Cerrado regions, which have severe environmental, economic, and social impacts.

The objective of this analysis is to explore historical wildfire data to uncover **patterns, trends, and possible contributing factors**, helping to support better **fire prevention and mitigation strategies**.

---

## Problem Statement
Forest fires in Brazil have increased over the years due to a combination of climatic conditions and human activities. Understanding:
- When fires occur most frequently
- Which regions are most affected
- How fire occurrences change over time  

is essential for data-driven environmental planning and policy-making.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze the **year-wise trend** of forest fires in Brazil  
- Identify **months and seasons** with the highest fire occurrences  
- Compare **state-wise fire distributions**  
- Detect patterns and anomalies in wildfire data  
- Support environmental awareness and risk assessment

3. **Univariate Analysis**

4. **Bivariate & Multivariate Analysis**

5. **Visualization**
